1. Roles

The Clinic is the data controller (responsable): it determines the purposes and means of processing its patients' personal data and issues the privacy notice to data subjects. Baja Dental AI is the data processor (encargado): it processes personal data solely on the Clinic's behalf and instructions, for the sole purpose of providing the Service. The processor will not use the data for its own purposes, will not sell it and will not disclose it to third parties other than the sub-processors listed in clause 6.

2. Data processed

Patient name and phone number; the audio recording and full transcript of the call; requested day and time, procedure of interest, language and travel origin. Possible sensitive data: the Service does not solicit clinical information — the assistant is prohibited from asking about symptoms, diagnosing or interpreting conditions — but a patient may volunteer references to pain, bleeding, swelling or infection, which are captured in the transcript and recorded as an emergency flag for handoff to a human. As such data may qualify as sensitive under article 3(VI) LFPDPPP, the parties agree to apply the reinforced measures in clause 4 and to limit it to the handoff purpose.

3. Authorized purposes

Only: (i) answering the call and capturing the appointment request; (ii) making the call record available to the Clinic; (iii) alerting Clinic staff to an urgent call; (iv) placing appointment reminder and confirmation calls; and (v) technical support for the foregoing. Any other purpose requires the Clinic's prior written instruction.

4. Security measures

Encryption in transit (TLS); per-clinic logical isolation enforced by row-level security policies in the database; restricted access to administrative credentials held solely by the processor; a recording disclosure at the start of every call in Spanish and English; and an assistant-level prohibition on issuing diagnoses, clinical advice, or claims about credentials the Clinic has not supplied.

5. Retention and return

Data is retained for the term of the Agreement. On termination the Clinic may request, in writing within thirty (30) calendar days, delivery of its data in a structured format. After that period the processor will delete the data save where legally required to retain it, confirming deletion in writing on request.

6. Sub-processors and international transfer

The Clinic authorizes Supabase (database and server functions), Vapi (voice and conversational AI) and Telnyx (telephony and SMS), all processing in the United States. The Clinic acknowledges this international transfer, which under article 37(V) LFPDPPP is necessary for performance of a contract concluded in the data subject's interest, and undertakes to reflect it in its own privacy notice. The processor will give at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice of any new or replacement sub-processor.

7. Data subject rights (ARCO)

Access, Rectification, Cancellation and Objection requests are received and decided by the Clinic as controller. The processor shall assist and carry out the corresponding instruction within five (5) business days of a written request.

8. Security breaches

The processor shall notify the Clinic of any security breach materially affecting data subjects' rights without undue delay and no later than seventy-two (72) hours after becoming aware of it, stating the nature of the incident, the data affected, corrective action taken and recommendations for data subjects. The Clinic, as controller, is responsible for notifying affected data subjects.

9. Confidentiality

The processor's and its personnel's confidentiality obligation survives termination.
