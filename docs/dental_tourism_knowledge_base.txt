BAJA DENTAL AI - PATIENT KNOWLEDGE BASE & FAQ
Use this document to answer patient questions regarding safety, costs, logistics, and procedures for traveling to Mexico for dental care.

### SECTION 1: SAFETY & STERILIZATION ###
Q: Is it safe to get dental work done in Mexico? Are the clinics clean?
A: Absolutely. Premium clinics in the Zona Médica of Mexicali strictly adhere to OSHA-level sterilization protocols, identical to those used in the United States and Canada. Our partner clinics feature state-of-the-art sterilization rooms, use autoclave technologies, and utilize advanced 3D CBCT imaging. Many of our specialists received their post-graduate training in the US or Europe and are board-certified. Safety and hygiene are our absolute highest priorities.

### SECTION 2: COST & QUALITY OF MATERIALS ###
Q: Why is the dental work so much cheaper in Mexico? Does cheap mean bad quality?
A: The cost is significantly lower (often 50% to 70% less than in the US) strictly due to economics, not quality. In Mexico, the cost of living, commercial real estate, and operational overhead are much lower. Additionally, Mexican dentists do not carry the massive burden of student debt or predatory malpractice insurance premiums that US dentists face. The materials used—such as Straumann or Nobel Biocare titanium implants, and premium zirconia crowns—are the exact same high-end brands used in Beverly Hills. You are paying for the procedure, not the overhead.

### SECTION 3: LOGISTICS & BORDER CROSSING ###
Q: Is Mexicali safe to travel to? How do I cross the border?
A: Mexicali is a major business and medical hub and is very welcoming to American tourists. The "Zona Médica" is highly secure and located just blocks from the US border crossing (Calexico/Mexicali). To make it entirely stress-free, our clinics offer VIP transportation. We can pick you up directly from the US side of the border or the airport, drive you to the clinic, and take you back. We also provide our patients with a "Medical Fast Pass" which allows you to use a dedicated VIP lane to cross back into the United States in a fraction of the usual time. You will need a valid US Passport or Passport Card to re-enter the US.

### SECTION 4: WARRANTIES & FOLLOW-UP CARE ###
Q: What happens if I have a problem after I go back home to California or the US? Is there a warranty?
A: We stand behind our work. Our partner clinics provide comprehensive warranties on major procedures, such as 5-year to lifetime guarantees on implant titanium posts, and warranties on zirconia crowns. If a minor adjustment is needed, many patients coordinate with a local dentist. However, if there is a complication related to the surgery or materials, we will fix it at no additional cost for the procedure itself. We always schedule thorough follow-up checks before you leave to ensure everything is healing perfectly.

### SECTION 5: TIMELINES & THE ALL-ON-4 PROCEDURE ###
Q: How long do I have to stay in Mexico for an All-on-4 implant procedure?
A: The All-on-4 procedure is typically done in two phases. 
Phase 1 (Surgery): You will need to stay for about 3 to 5 days. During this time, the doctor extracts failing teeth, places the titanium implants, and attaches a fixed, temporary set of teeth. You walk out with a beautiful smile.
Phase 2 (Finals): You will return home to heal for 4 to 6 months while the implants fuse to your bone. Then, you return to Mexicali for a 3-day trip where the doctor takes final impressions and attaches your permanent, custom-milled Zirconia teeth.

### SECTION 6: PAYMENT & INSURANCE ###
Q: Do you accept US dental insurance? How do I pay?
A: While Mexican clinics cannot directly bill US insurance companies, we will provide you with a fully itemized invoice with all the proper dental billing codes. You can submit this paperwork to your US insurance provider for reimbursement if your PPO plan covers out-of-network international care. For payment, clinics securely accept US cash, major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard), and sometimes cashier's checks.

### SECTION 7: CLINICAL PROCEDURES & PRICING ###
Q: What exactly is the All-on-4 or All-on-6 Implant procedure?
A: It is the ultimate permanent solution for patients missing all their teeth, or those struggling with dentures. The surgeon places 4 to 6 titanium implants directly into your jawbone to act as strong "roots." A permanent, full-arch bridge of natural-looking teeth is then screwed directly onto these implants. They never come out, you brush them like real teeth, and you regain 100% of your biting force. In the US, this costs $25,000 to $40,000 per arch. In Mexico, it is typically $10,000 to $12,000 per arch using the exact same premium titanium.

Q: What is the difference between Porcelain Veneers and Zirconia Crowns?
A: Veneers are ultra-thin ceramic shells (like a contact lens) that are bonded strictly to the front of your existing teeth to fix discoloration, chips, or gaps, giving you a flawless "Hollywood Smile." Zirconia Crowns, on the other hand, are full caps that encase the entire tooth to restore its structural integrity after severe decay, damage, or a root canal. In the US, premium veneers or crowns easily cost $1,500+ per tooth. In our clinics, they are typically $400 to $600 per tooth.

Q: Why shouldn't I just get Composite Veneers? They are cheaper.
A: Composite resin veneers are cheaper upfront, but they stain easily (from coffee or wine), chip frequently, and usually need to be replaced every 3 to 5 years. Porcelain and Zirconia veneers are highly resistant to staining and can easily last 15 to 20 years if maintained properly. They are the premium, permanent solution for a smile makeover.

Q: Do root canals hurt? How much do they cost?
A: Root canals have a bad reputation, but with modern anesthetics and our board-certified Endodontists, the procedure is entirely painless—most patients fall asleep! We remove the infected nerve inside the tooth to save it from extraction, then seal it. In the US, root canals often cost $1,200 to $1,800. In Mexico, they are usually between $300 and $450.

Q: Do you offer Invisalign or clear aligners?
A: Yes, we offer premium clear aligners like Invisalign to straighten your teeth without metal braces. However, because orthodontic treatment requires periodic check-ups to ensure your teeth are tracking properly, this treatment is best suited for patients who live relatively close to the border (like Southern California or Arizona) and can drive down every few months. In the US, Invisalign usually costs $5,000 to $7,000. Our clinics typically charge around $2,500 to $3,500.

Q: What is a Bone Graft and why might I need one for implants?
A: If you have been missing teeth for a long time, or have severe gum disease, your jawbone may have deteriorated. A bone graft is a routine procedure where the surgeon adds specialized bone material to your jaw to ensure there is a strong, solid foundation to anchor your titanium dental implants. It is a very common, painless procedure that is often performed on the exact same day your failing teeth are extracted.
